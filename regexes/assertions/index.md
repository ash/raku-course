title: Lookaround assertions
---

{% include menu.html %}

Sometimes you want to match something only because of what comes **next to** it, without making that neighbour part of the match. A _lookaround assertion_ checks the text ahead of or behind the current position but does not consume it.

There are two directions — _lookahead_ (what follows) and _lookbehind_ (what precedes) — and each comes in a positive and a negative form. Like anchors, assertions match a position, not characters.
